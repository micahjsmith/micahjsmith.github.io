Behind the scenes: consensual Jupyter telemetry for the Ballet case study

Posted on Fri 18 December 2020 in research

As part of our recently released paper on Ballet, a novel framework for collaborative data science development, we analyzed a case study of a collaboration to predict personal income from raw responses to the U.S. Census American Community Survey. We anticipated that many contributors to the project would use Assemble, a cloud-based development workflow for Ballet projects. In this post, I go "behind the scenes" of the research process to describe how we collected telemetry measurements from Assemble in order to better understand usage of this development environment in the context of a data science collaboration.
